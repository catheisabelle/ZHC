Unit Relationships: Kilo, Centi, Milli

What are Unit Relationships?
Unit relationships help us understand how different units are related to each other. For example, kilo, centi, and milli are special prefixes that tell us how big or small a unit is.

Kilo: 1,000
Kilo means 1,000. When you see 'kilo' in a word like kilogram or kilometer, it means 1,000 of that unit.

Examples:
  • 1 kilogram (kg) = 1,000 grams (g)
  • 1 kilometer (km) = 1,000 meters (m)
Centi: 1/100
Centi means 1/100, or one hundredth. It makes the unit smaller.

Examples:
  • 1 centimeter (cm) = 1/100 of a meter (m)
  • 1 centiliter (cl) = 1/100 of a liter (l)
Milli: 1/1,000
Milli means 1/1,000, or one thousandth. It makes the unit even smaller.

Examples:
  • 1 millimeter (mm) = 1/1,000 of a meter (m)
  • 1 milliliter (ml) = 1/1,000 of a liter (l)
