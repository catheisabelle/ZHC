Comparing, Ordering, and Estimating Numbers on a Number Line

What is a Number Line?
A number line is like a ruler with numbers marked on it. It helps us see where numbers are and how big or small they are compared to each other.

How to Compare and Order Numbers
Comparing Numbers:
To compare numbers on a number line, look at where the numbers are placed:
  • Example: On a number line from 0 to 50, 30 is to the right of 20, so 30 > 20.
Comparison Signs:
  • < means "less than"
  • > means "greater than"
Example:
  • 20 < 30 because 20 is smaller than 30.
Ordering Numbers:
To arrange numbers in order, see which number is farthest left (smallest) to farthest right (largest):
  • Example: On a line from 0 to 100: 25, 40, 75 → 25 < 40 < 75
Estimating Numbers on a Number Line
To estimate numbers, find the closest mark on the number line:
  • If the number isn't exactly on a mark, estimate where it should go.
Example:
  • If the number line goes from 0 to 100 and you’re looking for 37, place it between 30 and 40, closer to 40.
