Multiplying, Dividing, and Rounding Numbers

Multiplying and Dividing by 10 or 100
In this section, we'll learn how to multiply and divide by 10 and 100, as well as how to round numbers to the nearest 10 or 100.

Multiplying by 10 or 100:
  • When you multiply by 10, add a zero at the end of the number.
  • When you multiply by 100, add two zeros at the end.
Dividing by 10 or 100:
  • When you divide by 10, remove one zero or move the digits one place to the right.
  • When you divide by 100, remove two zeros or move the digits two places to the right.
Multiplying and Dividing by 10 or 100 Explained
Examples of Multiplying:
  • 43 × 10 = 430 (Add one zero at the end)
  • 25 × 100 = 2,500 (Add two zeros at the end)
Examples of Dividing:
  • 320 ÷ 10 = 32 (Remove one zero or move one place to the right)
  • 4,000 ÷ 100 = 40 (Remove two zeros or move two places to the right)
Rounding Numbers to the Nearest 10 or 100
Rounding to the Nearest 10:
  • Look at the ones place:
  • If it’s 5 or more, round up.
  • If it’s 4 or less, round down.
  • Examples: 47 → round up to 50, 32 → round down to 30
Rounding to the Nearest 100:
  • Look at the tens place:
  • If it’s 50 or more, round up.
  • If it’s 49 or less, round down.
  • Examples: 376 → round up to 400, 524 → round down to 500
