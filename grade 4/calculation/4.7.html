Ratios and Proportions

Understanding Ratios
What are ratios?
A ratio compares two numbers or amounts. It tells us how much of one thing there is compared to another.

Example: If you have 2 apples and 3 oranges, the ratio of apples to oranges is written as 2:3.
This means, for every 2 apples, there are 3 oranges.

Understanding Proportions
What are proportions?
A proportion is an equation that shows two ratios are equal.

Example: If you know the ratio of apples to oranges is 2:3, and you have 4 apples, how many oranges do you have?
Proportion: 2/3 = 4/x (where x is the number of oranges).
You can solve by cross-multiplying: 2 × x = 3 × 4 → 2x = 12 → x = 6.
So, you would have 6 oranges for 4 apples.

Working with Ratios
How to work with ratios:

If the ratio is 1:2, it means for every 1 of something, there are 2 of something else.
Example: If you have 3 red marbles, how many blue marbles would you have if the ratio is 1:2? (You would have 6 blue marbles.)

Working with Proportions
How to work with proportions:

Proportions show that ratios are equal. You can use them to find missing numbers.

Example: If the ratio of cats to dogs is 2:5, and you have 10 cats, how many dogs do you have?
Proportion: 2/5 = 10/x → 2x = 5 × 10 → 2x = 50 → x = 25.
You would have 25 dogs.
