Adding and Subtracting Three-Digit Numbers

Steps to Add Three-Digit Numbers
Step 1: Line up the numbers by place value
Write the numbers so the hundreds, tens, and ones are in columns.

Steps to Add Three-Digit Numbers
Step 2: Start adding from the ones place
Add the digits in the ones place first. If the total is 10 or more, carry over to the tens place.

Steps to Add Three-Digit Numbers
Step 3: Move to the tens place
Add the tens digits, including any number you carried over.

Steps to Add Three-Digit Numbers
Step 4: Finish with the hundreds place
Add the hundreds digits, including any carry-over.

Example: Adding Three-Digit Numbers
324 + 187:

Ones: 4 + 7 = 11 (write 1, carry 1).
Tens: 2 + 8 + 1 = 11 (write 1, carry 1).
Hundreds: 3 + 1 + 1 = 5.

Answer: 511

Steps to Subtract Three-Digit Numbers
Step 1: Line up the numbers by place value
Write the numbers so the hundreds, tens, and ones are in columns.

Steps to Subtract Three-Digit Numbers
Step 2: Start subtracting from the ones place
If the top number is smaller than the bottom number, borrow from the tens place.

Steps to Subtract Three-Digit Numbers
Step 3: Move to the tens place
Subtract the tens digits. If you borrowed, subtract 1 from the top tens digit.

Steps to Subtract Three-Digit Numbers
Step 4: Finish with the hundreds place
Subtract the hundreds digits.

Example: Subtracting Three-Digit Numbers
453 – 176:

Ones: 3 – 6 (borrow 1 from the tens, making it 13 – 6 = 7).
Tens: 4 – 7 (borrow 1 from the hundreds, making it 14 – 7 = 7).
Hundreds: 3 – 1 = 2.

Answer: 277
