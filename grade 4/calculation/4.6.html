Multiply and Divide Two-Digit Numbers

Steps to Multiply Two-Digit Numbers
Step 1: Set up the problem
Write the two-digit number on top and the single-digit number below.

Steps to Multiply Two-Digit Numbers
Step 2: Multiply the ones place
Multiply the digit in the ones place of the two-digit number by the single-digit number.

Example: 36 × 4 = (Multiply 6 by 4) → 6 × 4 = 24. Write 4 down, and carry over 2.

Steps to Multiply Two-Digit Numbers
Step 3: Multiply the tens place
Multiply the digit in the tens place of the two-digit number by the single-digit number. Add any carry-over from the ones place.

Example: 36 × 4 = (Multiply 3 by 4) → 3 × 4 = 12. Add the carry-over 2. So, 12 + 2 = 14. Answer: 36 × 4 = 144.

Example: Multiplying Two-Digit Numbers
36 × 4:

Ones: 6 × 4 = 24 (write 4, carry 2).
Tens: 3 × 4 + 2 = 14.

Answer: 36 × 4 = 144

Steps to Divide Two-Digit Numbers
Step 1: Set up the problem
Write the two-digit number (dividend) inside the division box, and the single-digit number (divisor) outside.

Steps to Divide Two-Digit Numbers
Step 2: Divide step-by-step
Start by dividing the first digit of the two-digit number by the single-digit number.

Example: 84 ÷ 4: How many times does 4 go into 8? The answer is 2. Write 2 above 8.

Steps to Divide Two-Digit Numbers
Step 3: Subtract and bring down the next digit
Subtract, and then bring down the next digit.

Example: 84 ÷ 4: 8 ÷ 4 = 2. Now subtract 8 – 8 = 0. Bring down the 4.

Steps to Divide Two-Digit Numbers
Step 4: Divide again
How many times does 4 go into 4? The answer is 1. Write 1 above the 4.

Example: 84 ÷ 4: The final answer is 21.

Example: Dividing Two-Digit Numbers
84 ÷ 4:

8 ÷ 4 = 2 (write 2 above 8).
Subtract 8 – 8 = 0, bring down the 4.
4 ÷ 4 = 1 (write 1 above 4).

Answer: 84 ÷ 4 = 21
