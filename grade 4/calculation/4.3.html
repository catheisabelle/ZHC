Multiplying and Dividing by 10

Step 1: Multiplying Single-Digit Numbers
When you multiply two single-digit numbers, you’re adding one number to itself many times.

Example:
3 × 4 means 3 added 4 times:
3 + 3 + 3 + 3 = 12.

So, 3 × 4 = 12.

Step 2: Multiplying Three-Digit Numbers by 10
When you multiply a number by 10, it gets 10 times bigger. You just add a zero to the end of the number.

Example:
125 × 10 = 1250.
The number 125 becomes 1250 when multiplied by 10.

Step 3: Dividing Three-Digit Numbers by 10
When you divide a number by 10, it gets 10 times smaller. You move the digits one place to the right or remove a zero if there’s one.

Example:
1250 ÷ 10 = 125.
The number 1250 becomes 125 when divided by 10.
