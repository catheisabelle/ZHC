Comparing and Ordering Angles

What are Angles?
An angle is the space between two lines that meet at a point, called the vertex. Angles are measured in degrees (°).
Right Angle (90º)
A right angle is an L-shape, like the corner of a piece of paper. Example: A square has four right angles.

Acute Angle (<90º)
An acute angle is smaller than a right angle. It’s sharp and pointy. Example: A 30º angle.

Obtuse Angle (>90º but <180º)
An obtuse angle is bigger than a right angle but smaller than a straight line. Example: A 120° angle.

Straight Angle (180º)
A straight angle is like a straight line, measuring exactly 180°. Example: A straight line is a straight angle.

How to Compare Angles
To compare angles, see which one opens more. The bigger the opening, the bigger the angle.

Example: An angle of 60° is smaller than an angle of 120°.
