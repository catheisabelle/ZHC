Nets for 3D Shapes

What are Nets for 3D Shapes?
A net is a flat shape that you can fold to make a 3D shape. It’s like cutting out a paper model of a 3D shape and then folding it to create the solid.

How to visualize and create nets:
Imagine cutting out a flat shape that, when folded, forms a 3D shape. For example, the net of a cube is made up of 6 squares arranged in a specific pattern.

Example 1 (Cube)
A cube has 6 square faces. The net for a cube looks like this: Imagine 4 squares in a row, with one square attached above the second square and another attached below the second square.

Example 2 (Pyramid)
A pyramid has 4 triangular faces and 1 square base. The net looks like a square in the middle with 4 triangles around it, forming the sides.

Example 3 (Cylinder)
A cylinder’s net is made of 2 circles (top and bottom) and 1 rectangle (the side). When you fold it, the rectangle wraps around to make the curved side of the cylinder, with the circles as the top and bottom.

How to Create a Net
Step 1: Draw the 2D shapes needed for the 3D object.
Step 2: Arrange them in a way that allows you to fold them into the 3D shape.
Step 3: Cut out the shapes and fold along the edges to see the 3D shape form.
