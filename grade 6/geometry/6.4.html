Plotting Coordinates and Transformations

Plotting Coordinates in All Four Quadrants
Coordinate Plane:
The coordinate plane is made up of two number lines: the x-axis (horizontal) and the y-axis (vertical).
The plane is divided into four quadrants:
  • Quadrant I: (+, +) — Both x and y are positive.
  • Quadrant II: (-, +) — x is negative, y is positive.
  • Quadrant III: (-, -) — Both x and y are negative.
  • Quadrant IV: (+, -) — x is positive, y is negative.

Plotting Points:
A coordinate is written as (x, y).
  • x is the horizontal value (left/right).
  • y is the vertical value (up/down).

Example:
  • (3, 4): Move 3 units right and 4 units up.
  • (-2, -3): Move 2 units left and 3 units down.
Predicting Transformations:
Reflection:
  • Reflection flips a shape over a line (mirror image).
  • Reflection over the x-axis: The y-coordinates change sign.
  • Reflection over the y-axis: The x-coordinates change sign.

Example:
  • Reflect point (3, 2) over the y-axis → (-3, 2).
Predicting Transformations:
Translation:
Translation slides a shape from one position to another without rotating or flipping it. The shape moves the same distance in the same direction.


Example:
  • Translate point (1, 2) by +3 on the x-axis and +2 on the y-axis → (4, 4).
Predicting Transformations:
Rotation:
Rotation turns a shape around a fixed point (usually the origin).
  • 90° clockwise rotation: Swap the x and y coordinates and change the sign of the new x-coordinate.
  • 180° rotation: Change the signs of both x and y coordinates.

Example:
  • Rotate point (3, 4) 90° clockwise → (4, -3).
  • Rotate point (3, 4) 180° → (-3, -4).
