Solving Problems with Fractions, Percentages, and Ratios

Let’s solve problems with fractions, percentages, and ratios!
These concepts help us understand parts of a whole, compare different values, and work with relationships between numbers.
Adding Fractions
To add fractions, they need to have the same denominator (bottom number). If they don’t, find a common denominator.
  • Example: 1/4 + 3/4 = 4/4 = 1
  • Example: 1/3 + 1/6: Find a common denominator (6). 1/3 = 2/6, so 2/6 + 1/6 = 3/6 = 1/2.
Multiplying Fractions
Multiply the numerators (top numbers) and the denominators (bottom numbers).
  • Example: 2/3 × 3/4 = (2 × 3) / (3 × 4) = 6/12 = 1/2
Dividing Fractions
Flip (find the reciprocal of) the second fraction and multiply.
  • Example: 2/3 ÷ 1/4 = 2/3 × 4/1 = 8/3
Finding a Percentage of a Number
To find a percentage of a number, convert the percentage to a decimal and multiply.
  • Example: Find 20% of 50: 20% = 0.20, 0.20 × 50 = 10
Converting Between Fractions, Decimals, and Percentages
Fraction to Percentage: Multiply the fraction by 100.
  • Example: 1/4 × 100 = 25%
Decimal to Percentage: Multiply the decimal by 100.
  • Example: 0.75 × 100 = 75%
Percentage to Decimal: Divide by 100.
  • Example: 25% = 0.25
Understanding Ratios
A ratio compares two numbers and shows how much of one there is compared to the other.
  • Example: The ratio of 3 apples to 2 oranges is written as 3:2.
Solving Ratio Problems
If you know one part of the ratio, use multiplication or division to find the others.
  • Example: If the ratio of apples to oranges is 3:2, and there are 12 apples, how many oranges are there?
  • 12 ÷ 3 = 4 (so there are 4 sets of 3 apples).
  • 4 × 2 = 8 oranges.
Scaling a Ratio
If you need to scale a ratio up or down, multiply or divide both parts of the ratio by the same number.
  • Example: The ratio of 2:3 can be scaled to 4:6 by multiplying both parts by 2.
