Multiplying, Dividing, and Rounding with 10, 100, and 1000

Let’s learn how to multiply and divide by 10, 100, and 1000, and how to round numbers and decimals!
These skills help us work with large and small numbers in math easily.
Multiplying by 10, 100, and 1000
Multiplying by 10: Move the decimal point one place to the right.
  • Example: 3 × 10 = 30
  • Example: 2.5 × 10 = 25.0

Multiplying by 100: Move the decimal point two places to the right.
  • Example: 4 × 100 = 400
  • Example: 3.2 × 100 = 320.0

Multiplying by 1000: Move the decimal point three places to the right.
  • Example: 5 × 1000 = 5000
  • Example: 1.75 × 1000 = 1750
Dividing by 10, 100, and 1000
Dividing by 10: Move the decimal point one place to the left.
  • Example: 30 ÷ 10 = 3
  • Example: 25 ÷ 10 = 2.5

Dividing by 100: Move the decimal point two places to the left.
  • Example: 400 ÷ 100 = 4
  • Example: 320 ÷ 100 = 3.2

Dividing by 1000: Move the decimal point three places to the left.
  • Example: 5000 ÷ 1000 = 5
  • Example: 1750 ÷ 1000 = 1.75
Rounding Whole Numbers
Steps to round whole numbers:
  • Look at the digit to the right of the place value you are rounding to.
  • If it’s 5 or higher, round up. If it’s 4 or lower, round down.

Example: Round 436 to the nearest ten.
Answer: 440 (because the 6 is higher than 5).

Rounding Decimals
To round a decimal to the nearest tenth: Look at the hundredths place.
Example: Round 3.67 to the nearest tenth.
Answer: 3.7 (because the 7 in the hundredths place is greater than 5).

To round to the nearest whole number: Look at the tenths place.
Example: Round 5.44 to the nearest whole number.
Answer: 5 (because the 4 in the tenths place is less than 5).
