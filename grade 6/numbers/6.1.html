Fractions, Decimals, and Large Numbers

Let’s explore fractions, decimals, and large numbers!
Fractions represent parts of a whole, decimals are another way to show parts, and large numbers are numbers with many digits that represent bigger amounts.
What are Fractions?
A fraction represents a part of a whole. It has two numbers: the numerator (top number) and the denominator (bottom number).

Example:
1/2 means 1 part out of 2 equal parts.
3/4 means 3 parts out of 4 equal parts.
Adding and Subtracting Fractions
You can add or subtract fractions if they have the same denominator. If they don’t, find a common denominator.

Examples:
1/4 + 2/4 = 3/4
1/2 + 1/3 = (3/6 + 2/6) = 5/6.
What are Decimals?
Decimals are another way to represent parts of a whole, using powers of 10 (like tenths, hundredths, etc.).

Examples:
0.5 means 5 tenths (or 1/2).
1.25 means 1 whole and 25 hundredths (or 1 and 25/100).
Adding and Subtracting Decimals
Line up the decimal points, and then add or subtract like normal.

Example:
3.2 + 1.5 = 4.7
Understanding Large Numbers
Large numbers are numbers with more digits, like 1,234 or 25,678.

Place Value Example:
In the number 12,345: The 1 is in the ten thousand place, the 2 is in the thousand place, the 3 is in the hundred place, and so on.
