Factors, Multiples, Odd/Even Numbers, Prime Numbers, and Ratios

Let’s learn about factors, multiples, odd/even numbers, prime numbers, and ratios!
These concepts are fundamental in math and help us understand the relationships between numbers.
Factors
A factor is a number that divides evenly into another number.
  • Example: Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12.
  • Why? Because 12 ÷ 1 = 12, 12 ÷ 2 = 6, 12 ÷ 3 = 4, 12 ÷ 4 = 3, 12 ÷ 6 = 2, 12 ÷ 12 = 1.
Multiples
A multiple is a number you get by multiplying a number by an integer (whole number).
  • Example: Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24…
  • These numbers are all the result of multiplying 4 by 1, 2, 3, 4, etc.
Odd and Even Numbers
Even Numbers: Even numbers are divisible by 2 and end in 0, 2, 4, 6, or 8.
  • Example: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10 are even numbers.

Odd Numbers: Odd numbers are not divisible by 2 and end in 1, 3, 5, 7, or 9.
  • Example: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9 are odd numbers.
Prime Numbers
A prime number is a number greater than 1 that has only two factors: 1 and itself.
  • Examples: 2 is prime (factors: 1, 2), 3 is prime (factors: 1, 3), 5 is prime (factors: 1, 5).
  • 4 is not prime because it has factors 1, 2, and 4.
Ratios
A ratio shows the relationship between two or more numbers. It tells how many times one number contains another.
  • Example: The ratio of 2 apples to 3 oranges is 2:3.
  • This means for every 2 apples, there are 3 oranges.
