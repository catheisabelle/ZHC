Divisibility Rules

Divisibility by 2
A number is divisible by 2 if its last digit is even (0, 2, 4, 6, or 8).

Examples:
  • 124 is divisible by 2 because its last digit (4) is even.
  • 135 is not divisible by 2 because its last digit (5) is odd.
Divisibility by 4
A number is divisible by 4 if the last two digits form a number divisible by 4.

Examples:
  • 512 is divisible by 4 because the last two digits (12) are divisible by 4.
  • 123 is not divisible by 4 because 23 is not divisible by 4.
Divisibility by 5
A number is divisible by 5 if its last digit is either 0 or 5.

Examples:
  • 35 is divisible by 5 because it ends in 5.
  • 42 is not divisible by 5 because it ends in 2.
Divisibility by 10
A number is divisible by 10 if its last digit is 0.

Examples:
  • 230 is divisible by 10 because it ends in 0.
  • 55 is not divisible by 10 because it does not end in 0.
Divisibility by 25
A number is divisible by 25 if its last two digits are either 00, 25, 50, or 75.

Examples:
  • 125 is divisible by 25 because it ends in 25.
  • 142 is not divisible by 25 because its last two digits (42) are not in 00, 25, 50, or 75.
Divisibility by 100
A number is divisible by 100 if its last two digits are 00.

Examples:
  • 500 is divisible by 100 because it ends in 00.
  • 980 is not divisible by 100 because it does not end in 00.
