Working with Fractions and Division

Fractions and Division
Fractions represent division:
The numerator (top number) is divided by the denominator (bottom number).
Example:
  • 1/2 means 1 ÷ 2 = 0.5.
  • 3/4 means 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75.
Finding Fractions of Quantities
How to Find a Fraction of a Quantity:
Step 1: Divide the quantity by the denominator (bottom number).
Step 2: Multiply the result by the numerator (top number).
Example:
  • Find 1/4 of 20:
  • Divide: 20 ÷ 4 = 5
  • Multiply: 5 × 1 = 5
  • Answer: 5
Relating Fractions to Division in Real-Life Contexts
Sharing Equally:
If you split 1 pizza between 2 people, each person gets 1/2 of the pizza, or 1 ÷ 2 = 0.5.

Finding a Fraction of an Amount:
If you have 40 candies and want to give 1/8 to a friend:
  • Divide: 40 ÷ 8 = 5
  • Multiply: 5 × 1 = 5 candies
Fractions as Division Example
Example:
If you have 3/5 of a cake and need to divide it into 5 equal parts:
  • First, divide the total quantity by the denominator: 3 ÷ 5 = 0.6
  • Now, to find the total cake each person would get: 0.6 × 1 = 0.6
  • Each part is 0.6 of the cake.
Dividing Quantities with Fractions
Dividing Quantities Using Fractions:
For example, if you have 15 marbles and want to divide them into 3 equal parts, you can express it as the fraction 1/3 of 15:
  • Divide: 15 ÷ 3 = 5
  • Multiply: 5 × 1 = 5 marbles per person
Working with Fractions in Real Life
Final Example:
If you have 100 apples and want to give 3/10 to a group of friends:
  • Divide: 100 ÷ 10 = 10
  • Multiply: 10 × 3 = 30 apples
  • You will give 30 apples to your friends.
