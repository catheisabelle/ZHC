Quick Calculations with Numbers and Decimals

Adding and Subtracting Numbers and Decimals
Whole Numbers: Simply add or subtract like normal.
  • Example: 45 + 32 = 77
  • Example: 85 - 27 = 58
Decimals: Line up the decimal points and add or subtract as usual.
  • Example: 3.4 + 2.6 = 6.0
  • Example: 5.7 - 2.3 = 3.4
Multiplying by 10, 100, and 1000
Multiplying by 10: Move the decimal point one place to the right.
  • Example: 5 × 10 = 50
  • Example: 0.7 × 10 = 7.0
Multiplying by 100: Move the decimal point two places to the right.
  • Example: 12 × 100 = 1200
  • Example: 1.25 × 100 = 125.0
Multiplying by 1000: Move the decimal point three places to the right.
  • Example: 8 × 1000 = 8000
  • Example: 0.75 × 1000 = 750.0
Dividing by 10, 100, and 1000
Dividing by 10: Move the decimal point one place to the left.
  • Example: 50 ÷ 10 = 5
  • Example: 2.5 ÷ 10 = 0.25
Dividing by 100: Move the decimal point two places to the left.
  • Example: 400 ÷ 100 = 4
  • Example: 5.6 ÷ 100 = 0.056
Dividing by 1000: Move the decimal point three places to the left.
  • Example: 3000 ÷ 1000 = 3
  • Example: 8.75 ÷ 1000 = 0.00875
