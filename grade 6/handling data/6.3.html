Probability and Likelihood of Events

What is Probability?
Probability:
Probability is the chance of an event happening. It is a way to measure how likely something is to occur. It is expressed as a fraction, decimal, or percentage between 0 and 1:
  • 0 means the event is impossible.
  • 1 means the event is certain.
  • Anything between 0 and 1 represents the likelihood of the event.
Example:
The probability of rolling a 3 on a standard 6-sided die is 1 out of 6 (1/6).

Basic Probability Terms
Certain: An event that will definitely happen.
Example: The sun will rise tomorrow.

Impossible: An event that cannot happen.
Example: Rolling a 7 on a standard 6-sided die.

Likely: An event that has a high chance of happening.
Example: It is likely to rain if the forecast predicts a 70% chance of rain.

Unlikely: An event that has a low chance of happening.
Example: Winning the lottery.

Equally Likely Outcomes

Example (Coin Flip):
  • A coin has 2 outcomes: heads or tails.
  • Probability of heads = 1 out of 2 = 1/2.
  • Probability of tails = 1 out of 2 = 1/2.
Assessing Likelihood
You can estimate the likelihood of an event using words like likely, unlikely, certain, and impossible.

Example (Red Card from a Deck):
  • A standard deck of cards has 52 cards. There are 26 red cards (hearts and diamonds).
  • Probability of drawing a red card = 26 รท 52 = 1/2.
  • Likelihood: It's equally likely to draw a red or black card.
