Sort, name, describe and draw 3D shapes

What are 3D shapes?
3D shapes are solid shapes that you can hold and touch. They have length, width, and height.
Common 3D shapes:
Cube:
  • A cube has 6 square faces that are all the same size.
  • Example: A dice is a cube!
Common 3D shapes:
Cuboid:
  • A cuboid looks like a box, and it has 6 rectangular faces.
  • Example: A box or a book is a cuboid!
Common 3D shapes:
Cylinder:
  • A cylinder has 2 round faces (at the top and bottom) and 1 curved face around the sides.
  • Example: A soda can is a cylinder!
Common 3D shapes:
Cone:
  • A cone has 1 flat circular face at the bottom and a pointy tip at the top.
  • Example: An ice cream cone is a cone!
Common 3D shapes:
Sphere:
  • A sphere is round and smooth, with no flat faces and no edges.
  • Example: A ball is a sphere!
Common 3D shapes:
Pyramid:
  • A pyramid has a square or triangular base and 4 triangular faces that meet at a point.
  • Example: A pyramid-shaped building or the Great Pyramid of Giza!
