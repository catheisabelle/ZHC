Recognize turns

What is a turn?
A turn is when you change direction. It’s like when you turn your body to face a different way.
Types of turns:
Whole turn (360º):
  • A whole turn means turning all the way around, like a full circle, until you’re facing the same way again.
  • Example: If you turn all the way around, you will end up in the same position you started from.

  • Clockwise: Turning to the right (the same way the hands on a clock move).
  • Anti-clockwise: Turning to the left (the opposite way the clock hands move).
Types of turns:
Half turn (180º):
  • A half turn means turning halfway around, like turning to face the opposite direction.
  • Example: If you’re facing forward and you make a half turn, you’ll be facing the other way.
Types of turns:
Quarter turn (90º):
  • A quarter turn is a small turn, like turning ¼ of the way around.
  • It’s like turning from facing forward to facing either left or right.

  • Clockwise: Turning to the right by 90°.
  • Anti-clockwise: Turning to the left by 90°.
