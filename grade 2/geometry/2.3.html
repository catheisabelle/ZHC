Identify reflective symmetry and draw lines of symmetry in 2D shapes

What is reflective symmetry & lines of symmetry?
Reflective symmetry means that if you fold a shape in half, both sides will match exactly. Itâ€™s like looking in a mirror! A line of symmetry is the line you would fold the shape along to make both sides match.
Shapes with reflective symmetry:
Circle:
  • A circle has many lines of symmetry because you can fold it in many ways and the sides will always match.
Shapes with reflective symmetry:
Square:
  • A square has 4 lines of symmetry! You can fold it horizontally, vertically, or along the two diagonals.
Shapes with reflective symmetry:
Rectangle:
  • A rectangle has 2 lines of symmetry: one vertical and one horizontal.
Shapes with reflective symmetry:
Equilateral triangle:
  • An equilateral triangle has 3 lines of symmetry, one going through each corner.
Shapes with reflective symmetry:
Heart:
  • A heart has 1 line of symmetry down the middle.
How to draw lines of symmetry:
  • To find the line of symmetry, fold the shape in half in your mind or use a ruler to draw a line where both sides match.
  • Example: If you draw a square, draw a vertical line down the middle. Then, fold it to check if both sides match.
