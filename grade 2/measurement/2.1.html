Estimate and Measure in Standard Units

Estimate
When you estimate, you make a good guess about how long, heavy, or full something is.
Example: This pencil is about 10 centimeters long.
Measure
When you measure, you use a tool to find out exactly how long, heavy, or full something is.
Example: Use a ruler to measure the pencil and check if itâ€™s really 10 centimeters.
Standard units and how to measure them:
Centimeters (cm) and meters (m) - length
Centimeters and meters are used to measure how long or tall something is.
  • Tool to measure it: ruler and measuring tape
  • Example: Your book is 20 cm long, and the classroom is about 5 meters long.
Standard units and how to measure them:
Kilograms (kg) and grams (g) - weight
Kilograms and grams are used to measure how heavy something is.
  • Tool to measure it: weighing scale
  • Example: A bag of sugar weighs 1 kilogram, and a chocolate bar weighs 100 grams.
Standard units and how to measure them:
Liters (l) - capacity
Liters are used to measure how much liquid is in a container.
  • Tool to measure it: measuring cup
  • Example: A bottle of water is 1 liter
