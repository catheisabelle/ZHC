Perimeter of Polygons and Area of Rectangles

Let’s learn about calculating the perimeter of polygons and the area of rectangles!
Perimeter is the total distance around a shape, and area is the amount of space inside a shape.
Perimeter of Polygons
The perimeter is the total distance around the outside of a shape. To find it, add the lengths of all the sides.
  • For a triangle with sides 5 cm, 7 cm, and 8 cm:
    Perimeter = 5 + 7 + 8 = 20 cm.
  • For a square with sides of 6 cm:
    Perimeter = 6 × 4 = 24 cm.
Area of Rectangles
Area is the amount of space inside a shape. For rectangles, the formula is: Area = length × width.

Example: For a rectangle with a length of 8 cm and a width of 5 cm:
  • Area = 8 × 5 = 40 cm².
