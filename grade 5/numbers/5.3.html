Multiply, Divide, and Round Numbers

Let’s learn how to multiply, divide, and round numbers!
These are useful skills that help us work with numbers more easily.
How to Multiply by 10
When you multiply a number by 10, you add one zero at the end.
Example: 5 × 10 = 50.

How to Multiply by 100
When you multiply a number by 100, you add two zeros at the end.
Example: 7 × 100 = 700.

How to Divide by 10
When you divide by 10, you move the decimal point one place to the left.
Example: 50 ÷ 10 = 5.

How to Divide by 100
When you divide by 100, you move the decimal point two places to the left.
Example: 700 ÷ 100 = 7.

How to Round Numbers to the Nearest 10
  1. Look at the ones place (the last digit).
  2. If it’s 5 or higher, round up.
  3. If it’s 4 or lower, round down.
    4. Examples:
    • 47 rounded to the nearest 10 is 50 (because the 7 is more than 5).
    • 34 rounded to the nearest 10 is 30 (because the 4 is less than 5).
How to Round Numbers to the Nearest 100
  1. Look at the tens place.
  2. If it’s 50 or higher, round up to the next hundred.
  3. If it’s 49 or lower, round down to the previous hundred.
Examples:
  • 145 rounded to the nearest 100 is 100 (because the 4 is less than 50).
  • 654 rounded to the nearest 100 is 700 (because the 5 is more than 4).
How to Round Numbers to the Nearest Whole Number
  1. Look at the first digit after the decimal.
  2. If it’s 5 or higher, round up.
  3. If it’s 4 or lower, round down.
Examples:
  • 8.6 rounded to the nearest whole number is 9.
  • 4.3 rounded to the nearest whole number is 4.
