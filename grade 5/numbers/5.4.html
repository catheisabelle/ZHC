These skills help us understand how numbers relate to each other.
How to Compare Whole Numbers
  • To compare whole numbers, look at the leftmost digits.
  • The larger number is the one with the bigger digit in that place.
Example: 50 is greater than 30 because 5 is bigger than 3.

How to Compare Negative Numbers
  • For negative numbers, the smaller the number, the more negative it is.
  • The number farther from zero is smaller.
Example: -7 is less than -3 because -7 is farther from zero.

How to Compare Decimal Numbers
  • To compare decimals, start from the left and compare each place value (tenths, hundredths, thousandths).
  • The number with the higher digit in the first place that differs is greater.
Example: 3.56 is greater than 3.45 because 5 is greater than 4 in the hundredths place.

How to Compare Fractions
  • If the denominators are the same, the fraction with the bigger numerator is greater.
Example: 3/5 is greater than 2/5 because 3 is greater than 2.

How to Order Whole Numbers
  • To order whole numbers from smallest to largest, list them by their place value.
Example: Order 8, 3, 15, 7 from smallest to largest: 3, 7, 8, 15.

How to Order Negative Numbers
  • Order negative numbers by their distance from zero. The number closer to zero is larger.
Example: Order -2, -5, -3, -1 from smallest to largest: -5, -3, -2, -1.

How to Order Decimal Numbers
  • Order decimals by comparing digits from left to right.
Example: Order 0.6, 0.55, 0.7, 0.65 from smallest to largest: 0.55, 0.6, 0.65, 0.7.

How to Order Fractions
  • If the fractions have different denominators, convert them to the same denominator or convert them to decimals to compare.
Example: Order 1/2, 2/3, 3/4 from smallest to largest: 1/2, 2/3, 3/4.
