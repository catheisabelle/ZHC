Reading Analogue and Digital Clocks

Let’s learn how to read both analogue and digital clocks!
Analogue clocks show time with hands on a face, and digital clocks show time with numbers. You’ll learn how to read both types of clocks.
Reading Analogue Clocks
Analogue clock: An analogue clock has a face with numbers and hands that move around it. There are two hands: the short hand (hour hand) and the long hand (minute hand).

How to read an analogue clock:
  • Hour Hand (Short Hand): This tells you the hour, which moves slowly.
  • Minute Hand (Long Hand): This tells you the minutes, and it moves faster.
  • Divisions: The clock is divided into 12 parts, and each part is 5 minutes.

Examples:
  • 5:25: The hour hand points just past 5, and the minute hand points to the 5 (for 25 minutes).
  • 3:40: The hour hand is close to 3 but not quite there, and the minute hand points to 8 (for 40 minutes).
Reading Digital Clocks
Digital clock: A digital clock shows the time using numbers, like 12:30 or 3:45.

How to read a digital clock:
  • Hours: The first number tells you the hour.
  • Minutes: The second number tells you the minutes.

Examples:
  • 2:45: The hour is 2, and the minutes are 45.
  • 6:10: The hour is 6, and the minutes are 10.
