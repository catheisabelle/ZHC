Units and Conversions

Let’s learn about units and conversions!
Measurement helps us find out how long, heavy, or full something is using the right tools.
Length (how long or tall something is)
  • Units: Centimeters (cm), Meters (m)
  • Tools: Ruler, Measuring Tape, Meter Stick
Example: Use a ruler to measure a pencil in centimeters.

Mass (how heavy something is)
  • Units: Grams (g), Kilograms (kg)
  • Tools: Weighing Scale
Example: A loaf of bread weighs about 500 grams.

Capacity (how much liquid fits in a container)
  • Units: Milliliters (ml), Liters (l)
  • Tools: Measuring Jug, Measuring Cup
Example: A small juice box holds 250 milliliters.

Key Conversions
Length:
  • 1 kilometer (km) = 1000 meters (m)
Example: If you walk 1 km, it’s the same as walking 1000 m.


Mass:
  • 1 kilogram (kg) = 1000 grams (g)
Example: If a watermelon weighs 1 kg, it’s the same as 1000 g.


Capacity:
  • 1 liter (l) = 1000 milliliters (ml)
Example: If a bottle holds 1 liter of water, it’s the same as 1000 ml.
