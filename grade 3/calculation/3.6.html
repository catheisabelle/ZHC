Halving, Doubling, and Remainders

Let’s Learn About Halving, Doubling, and Remainders!
Halving, doubling, and dealing with remainders are important math skills. Let's explore what these mean with examples!
Halving
Halving means splitting a number into 2 equal parts.

Example: Half of 12 is 6 because 12 ÷ 2 = 6.

Doubling
Doubling means adding a number to itself.

Example: Double 6 is 12 because 6 + 6 = 12.

Remainders
Remainders are what’s left over after dividing a number.

Example: If you divide 10 by 3, you get 3 groups of 3, but 1 is left over. So, 10 ÷ 3 = 3 remainder 1.
