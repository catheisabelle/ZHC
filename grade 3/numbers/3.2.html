Count in 1s, 10s, 100s, and steps of 2, 3, 4, 5

Let’s Learn to Count!
Counting means naming numbers in the correct order. You can count by 1s, 10s, 100s, or in steps of 2, 3, 4, and 5 to make counting faster!
Counting in 1s
Counting by 1s means counting one number at a time.

Here’s how:

Start at 1 and keep going: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5… all the way to 1000!

Counting in 10s
Counting by 10s means adding 10 each time.

Here’s how:

Start at 10 and count by tens: 10, 20, 30, 40, 50… all the way to 1000!

Counting in 100s
Counting by 100s means adding 100 each time.

Here’s how:

Start at 100 and count by hundreds: 100, 200, 300, 400… all the way to 1000!

Counting in Steps of 2
Counting by 2 means adding 2 each time.

Here’s how:

Start at 2 and count by twos: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10… all the way up!

Counting in Steps of 3
Counting by 3 means adding 3 each time.

Here’s how:

Start at 3 and count by threes: 3, 6, 9, 12… all the way up!

Counting in Steps of 4
Counting by 4 means adding 4 each time.

Here’s how:

Start at 4 and count by fours: 4, 8, 12, 16… all the way up!

Counting in Steps of 5
Counting by 5 means adding 5 each time.

Here’s how:

Start at 5 and count by fives: 5, 10, 15, 20… all the way up!
