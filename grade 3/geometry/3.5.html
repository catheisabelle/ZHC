Clockwise and Anti-clockwise Movement

Let’s learn about clockwise and anti-clockwise movement!
Clockwise means moving in the same direction as the hands of a clock, and anti-clockwise means moving the opposite direction.
Clockwise Movement
Clockwise: Moving in the same direction as the hands of a clock.

Example: If you point up and turn to the right, you’re moving clockwise.

Anti-clockwise Movement
Anti-clockwise: Moving in the opposite direction of the hands of a clock.

Example: If you point up and turn to the left, you’re moving anti-clockwise.

Describing Movement
To describe movement:
  1. Clockwise: Turn your body to the right in a circle. That’s moving clockwise.
  2. Anti-clockwise: Now turn your body to the left in a circle. That’s moving anti-clockwise.
Describing Position
To describe position:
  • If someone is standing to your right, they are clockwise from you.
  • If someone is standing to your left, they are anti-clockwise from you.
