Comparing Angles with Right Angle

Let’s learn about comparing angles with a right angle!
A right angle is exactly 90 degrees. Let's compare it to other angles:
  • Acute angle: Smaller than a right angle (less than 90°).
  • Obtuse angle: Larger than a right angle (greater than 90°).
Right Angle
A right angle is an angle of 90 degrees. It forms an "L" shape, like the corner of a square or a piece of paper.

Example: A right angle is exactly 90°.

Acute Angle
An acute angle is smaller than a right angle. It is any angle less than 90°.

Example: An acute angle might be 45°.

Obtuse Angle
An obtuse angle is larger than a right angle but smaller than 180°. It is any angle between 90° and 180°.

Example: An obtuse angle might be 120°.
