Data is information we collect to help us learn about something, like how many mini-beasts (bugs) live in different areas of your garden.
Collecting Data
Step 1: Gather Information
For example, you might count how many bugs you find in different parts of your garden, like near the flowers or under the tree.

Example:
"Count the number of ants near the flowers and under the tree in your garden."

Organizing Data
Step 2: Organize the Data
After collecting the data, you need to organize it so it's easy to understand. You can use a tally chart or a table to write down your findings.

Example:
Create a tally chart to show how many ants you found near the flowers and under the tree.

Interpreting Data
Step 3: Interpret the Data
Now, look at the data you have collected and organized. Think about what it means.

Example:
If you have more ants near the flowers than under the tree, you might interpret that ants prefer flowers.
